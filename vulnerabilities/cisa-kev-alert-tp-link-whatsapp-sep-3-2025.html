Critical Alert: CISA Adds Actively Exploited TP-Link and WhatsApp Flaws to KEV Catalog

üóìÔ∏è 3 September 2025

On Sept 2, CISA added the actively exploited TP-Link TL-WA855RE (CVE-2020-24363) and a chained WhatsApp flaw (CVE-2025-55177) to its KEV catalog. See the urgent EoL hardware advisory and FCEB deadline.

High-Risk TP-Link Flaw Under Active Attack

The U.S. Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency (CISA) has flagged the **TP-Link TL-WA855RE Wi-Fi Range Extender** for a dangerous vulnerability, **CVE-2020-24363** (CVSS score: 8.8), due to confirmed exploitation in the wild. This high-severity flaw stems from a **missing authentication** issue. An unauthenticated attacker on the same local network can exploit this by submitting a specific POST request (`TDDP_RESET`) to force a factory reset and reboot. Once reset, the attacker can seize administrative control by setting a new administrative password, leading to complete device compromise.

Critical User Advisory

While the issue was reportedly fixed in firmware version **TL-WA855RE(EU)_V5_200731**, the TL-WA855RE product line has reached **End-of-Life (EoL) status**. This means it will not receive any further security patches or updates. **The most secure recommendation for users of the vulnerable extender is to immediately replace the device with a newer, supported model** to eliminate a permanent, high-risk entry point into their network.

WhatsApp Flaw Chained in Targeted Spyware Campaign

In addition to the hardware vulnerability, CISA also added a critical WhatsApp security flaw, **CVE-2025-55177** (CVSS score: 5.4), to the KEV catalog. This vulnerability, which involves **incomplete authorization** of linked device synchronization messages, was exploited as part of a highly-targeted commercial spyware operation. Attackers leveraged this WhatsApp flaw by chaining it with another vulnerability in Apple's operating systems (**CVE-2025-43300**, CVSS score: 8.8) targeting iOS, iPadOS, and macOS devices for full, zero-click compromise. WhatsApp confirmed the campaign was highly selective, notifying fewer than 200 users via in-app threat warnings that their devices may have been targeted.

Mandatory Mitigation Deadline

Federal Civilian Executive Branch (FCEB) agencies are under a strict directive to apply the necessary mitigations for both **CVE-2020-24363** and **CVE-2025-55177** by **September 23, 2025**, to counteract these active threats. For all organizations and users, this CISA alert underscores the critical importance of immediately updating vulnerable applications and operating systems and promptly retiring End-of-Life (EoL) hardware that can no longer be secured against known, actively exploited vulnerabilities.
