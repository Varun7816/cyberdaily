Google releases September 2025 Android updates, fixing 120 flaws including CVE-2025-38352 (Linux Kernel) and CVE-2025-48543 (Runtime), both actively exploited zero-days.

Two Zero-Days Under Active Exploitation

As part of its routine monthly cycle, Google has released the September 2025 security update for the Android platform, addressing a substantial total of **120 vulnerabilities**. Most critically, the bulletin flags two distinct **privilege escalation** flaws that the company confirms were already being actively exploited in limited, targeted attacks before a patch was available. Neither flaw requires user interaction for exploitation, meaning a local attacker can gain higher access permissions on a device without any additional privileges.

Critical Vulnerability Details

The two vulnerabilities under attack are: * **CVE-2025-38352** (CVSS: 7.4): A local privilege escalation bug residing in the fundamental **Linux Kernel** component. * **CVE-2025-48543** (CVSS: N/A): A local privilege escalation issue affecting the core **Android Runtime** component.
